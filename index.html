Major Brands with Documented History

Airmail / Wality

Airmail pens, known internationally by their export name Wality, are among the oldest pen manufacturers in India, established in 1951. Based in Mumbai, the company is recognized for its classic, handmade fountain pens, particularly the durable eyedropper fillers made from ebonite. The name "Wality" was adopted for international sales to avoid a distribution conflict within India.

Where to Buy

Wality/Airmail pens can be found on various Indian and international fountain pen websites.

ASA

Founded in Chennai in 2013 by L. Subramaniam, ASA Pens is a brand revered by fountain pen enthusiasts. The name is an acronym from the founder's family members. Subramaniam, a passionate pen collector, started the company to create high-quality, handcrafted pens, primarily from ebonite, making the art of Indian pen-making more accessible.

Where to Buy

You can purchase ASA pens directly from their official website, or through a number of specialized Indian and international retailers.

Brahmam

Brahmam pens are part of the esteemed legacy of the "House of Ratnam." Inspired by Mahatma Gandhi's call for indigenous products, K.V. Ratnam and his cousin, K.V. Brahmam, started their pen-making ventures. K.V. Brahmam founded the brand in Bhimavaram, Andhra Pradesh, and it shares a deep history with the Ratnam family.

Where to Buy

These pens are generally available from small-scale retailers or directly from the family workshop in Andhra Pradesh.

Camlin

Camlin is one of India's most iconic stationery brands, with a history that begins in 1931 when two brothers, D.P. and G.P. Dandekar, started a company to manufacture ink. Initially named "Camel," the brand later became "Camlin" (a blend of "Camel" and "Ink") as it expanded its product range. In 2011, Japanese company Kokuyo acquired the brand, forming Kokuyo Camlin Ltd.

Where to Buy

Camlin products are ubiquitous in India, available in virtually all stationery stores and on all major e-commerce platforms.

Cello

Founded in 1967, the Cello Group first made its mark with plastic housewares before venturing into pens in 1995. Cello quickly became a market leader with its affordable and reliable writing instruments. In 2009, the French company BIC acquired a major stake, and the writing division was later rebranded as BIC Cello (India).

Where to Buy

Cello pens are widely available across India in stationery shops and on all major online retail sites.

Chelpark

Chelpark, a portmanteau of "Chellaram" and "Parker," was originally the Indian subsidiary of the Parker Pen Company, founded in 1943. It was highly regarded for its high-quality, anti-clogging fountain pen inks and classic pen models like the "Moti" and "Sona." The company ceased pen and ink manufacturing in the 2010s.

Where to Buy

Chelpark products are vintage and can be found on online auction sites and from pen collectors.

Click

Click Pens was established in 1978 in Indore by Gokuldas Gagwani, a veteran of the pen manufacturing industry. The company's mission was to produce quality writing instruments at reasonable prices. Today, under the leadership of his grandson, Harsh Gagwani, the brand is well-known in the fountain pen community for its handcrafted ebonite pens.

Where to Buy

You can buy Click pens from their official website and various Indian and international online pen retailers.

Flair

Flair Writing Industries Limited is one of India's largest writing instrument manufacturers, incorporated in 1976. The company is known for its extensive portfolio of products, including ballpoint, gel, and fountain pens, all aimed at the mass market and known for their affordability.

Where to Buy

Flair pens are widely available in India in stationery stores and on all major e-commerce sites.

Fountain Pen Revolution (FPR)

FPR is a global brand with its pens manufactured in India. Founded by Kevin and Nicole Thorp, the company is celebrated for its affordable pens and flexible nibs. They have a strong connection to Indian pen-making traditions, collaborating with local manufacturers to produce their pens.

Where to Buy

FPR pens are primarily sold through their own official website.

Gama

Based in Guntur, Andhra Pradesh, Gama Pens is renowned for its large, handcrafted ebonite fountain pens. As part of a long-standing tradition of pen-making in the region, their pens are favored by enthusiasts for their simple, robust construction and classic eyedropper-filler design.

Where to Buy

Gama pens can be found on their official website and other online retailers specializing in Indian fountain pens.

Guider

Guider is another brand from the pen-making hub of Rajahmundry, Andhra Pradesh. The brand, run by the family of Mr. L. Suryanarayana Rao, has a long history of making handcrafted ebonite fountain pens and is known for its traditional designs and high-quality craftsmanship.

Where to Buy

Guider pens are available directly from the manufacturer and a few specialized online retailers.

Krishna Pens

Krishna Pens is a modern Indian brand that has gained a strong reputation for its line of boutique fountain pen inks, which are highly regarded by the international fountain pen community. The brand also produces pens, focusing on quality and unique designs.

Where to Buy

Krishna pens and inks are sold on a variety of specialized online pen stores.

Luxor

Luxor Writing Instruments, founded in 1963, is a significant name in the Indian stationery industry. It has a history of collaborating with international brands like Pilot and Parker to manufacture and distribute their products in India, in addition to producing its own range of pens.

Where to Buy

Luxor pens are widely available at stationery stores nationwide and on all major e-commerce platforms.

Mhatre / Plato

Mhatre pens and their sub-brand, Plato, are part of the legacy of a Mumbai-based pen-making family. These vintage and collectible pens, popular in the mid-20th century, are known for their classic and simple designs. The brand is no longer in operation.

Where to Buy

These pens are vintage and are only available from collectors or on online auction sites.

Montex

Montex is an established Indian pen brand that has been in the market for several decades. They are known for producing a wide range of affordable and reliable ballpoint and gel pens for the mass market.

Where to Buy

Montex pens are available at stationery stores across India and on various online marketplaces.

Oliver

A vintage Indian pen brand from the mid-20th century, Oliver was a prominent manufacturer based in Mumbai. They produced a variety of pens and were known for their quality craftsmanship. The brand is now defunct.

Where to Buy

Oliver pens are vintage and are only available from collectors and on online auction sites.

Pilot India

While Pilot is a Japanese company, its presence in India is largely due to its partnership with Luxor. Luxor has been the exclusive partner for manufacturing and distributing Pilot pens in India, which has made popular models widely available.

Where to Buy

Pilot pens are available at stationery stores nationwide and on all major e-commerce websites.

Ranga

Ranga Pen Company, a family-owned business in Thiruvallur, Tamil Nadu, has a legacy of producing handcrafted ebonite fountain pens. Run by Mr. M.S. Pandurangan, the brand has gained a strong international reputation for its beautiful, well-made pens and a wide range of custom designs.

Where to Buy

Ranga pens are available directly from their official website and through various specialized online retailers.

Ratnam / Ratnamson

The name Ratnam is a legend in Indian pen history. The company was founded by K.V. Ratnam in Rajahmundry in 1932, in response to a call from Mahatma Gandhi to produce a "Swadeshi" (indigenous) pen. These were the first Indian-made fountain pens. The family continues this legacy, with some members operating under the original "Ratnam" name and others as "Ratnamson," all known for their custom handmade pens.

Where to Buy

These pens are primarily available by contacting the manufacturers directly through their websites or social media pages.

Serwex

Serwex is a brand from Delhi with a history of producing affordable fountain pens. They were a common sight in Indian schools for decades, known for their student-friendly models. The brand is still in operation, though perhaps with a smaller presence than in its heyday.

Where to Buy

Serwex pens can be found from some Indian online retailers specializing in fountain pens.

Kanwrite

Kanwrite is a popular brand known for its affordable and high-quality fountain pens, especially in the student market. They are also well-regarded for their flexible nibs.

Kim & Co. is a well-known brand among students for its affordable and reliable fountain pens. They are a popular choice for daily use and are easily accessible in local markets.

Lotus Pens

Based in Uttar Pradesh, Lotus Pens is a newer brand that has quickly gained a reputation for its beautiful handcrafted pens. They use materials like ebonite and acrylic to create unique, high-quality designs for enthusiasts.
